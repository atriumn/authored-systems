Placeholder. The complexity/enterprise objection.

Key points:
- Walk through SDLC: deploys, incident response, on-call, change management — all six primitives
- Walk through product dev: discovery, specs, launches, retros — all six
- Walk through HR: hiring, PIPs, comp reviews, onboarding — all six
- The more complex the operation, the higher the Heroism Tax
- Simple operations can survive on tribal knowledge (a 3-person team can just talk)
- Complex operations cannot — which is why healthcare, aviation, and nuclear authored their systems
- The six primitives don't add complexity. They name the complexity that already exists.
- Celonis stat: 85% of enterprises want agentic AI, 76% can't support it. That's the complexity objection in reverse.
