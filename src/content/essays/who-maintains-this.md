Placeholder. The maintenance/staleness objection.

Key points:
- Policies go stale in every system — the question is whether you notice
- In an inferred system, stale policies are invisible — they just drift
- In an authored system, the Ledger detects drift: "this policy triggered 40 false escalations last month"
- AI proposes changes, humans approve — the feedback loop is built in
- Version history on every policy and procedure — you can see what changed and why
- The Operations Architect role: someone owns the system, not just the tools
- Maintenance is continuous and small, not periodic and massive
